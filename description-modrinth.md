## What is Civillis?

Have you ever wished your base just *felt* safe ‚Äî without flooding every surface with torches or carpeting the ground with slabs?

Civillis makes that happen. It recognizes the signs of civilization you naturally create ‚Äî a campfire burning, a bed placed, a workbench humming ‚Äî and silently pushes hostile mobs away. The more your settlement grows, the stronger the protection. No commands, no rituals, no new mechanics to learn ‚Äî just play, and the land responds.

Leave for too long, and nature slowly reclaims its ground. But a well-established city never truly falls.

## How It Works

The mod evaluates a 240√ó240√ó48 block area (configurable) around each potential spawn point and computes a **civilization score** based on nearby structures. Blocks that reflect human presence ‚Äî furnaces, beds, campfires, beacons, workstations, and others like them ‚Äî are what the mod looks for.

- **Settle and grow ‚Üí safer land.** A thriving city can push monsters back ~90 blocks from its borders.
- **Leave for a while ‚Üí gradual decay.** Over time without visits, protection weakens ‚Äî but a large settlement still keeps a ~40-block safe perimeter.
- **A small cabin ‚Üí modest protection.** A lone outpost won't create a fortress, but it helps.

*That's millions of blocks evaluated per spawn attempt ‚Äî and it happens every time, everywhere in the world. See the [Wiki](https://maoxnz.github.io/Civillis/) to learn how the shard-based civilization engine keeps this at constant time.*

Want danger back? Place a monster skull to override the protection. A skeleton skull increases the chance of skeletons spawning nearby, a zombie head draws more zombies, and so on. Stack three or more skulls in an area and mobs start converting into the types you've placed. Mix and match to fine-tune the threats you face.

Craft a detector from a compass surrounded by emeralds, and scan your local civilization level. Trigger a sonar pulse that sweeps the land around you. Color-coded boundaries and custom sound cues tell you exactly how safe (or exposed) your surroundings are.

The world doesn't stay tamed forever. Unvisited areas gradually lose their protection over time, creating a living, breathing sense of territory that rewards active presence. Offline time in singleplayer doesn't count ‚Äî your civilization picks up right where you left off.

### üîß Mod & Modpack Friendly
Ships with built-in scoring for blocks from Farmer's Delight, Supplementaries, and Create ‚Äî with more mods added over time. All block weights and head types are loaded from JSON data files, fully overridable via datapacks. Mods that add custom skull types are automatically compatible.
